The best place to rent in large cities¶

Large cities are popular destinations for tourists. However, renting a hotel room in such a city is expensive. Airbnb provide a cheaper option, especially if you are willing to live outside of the city center.

The web page provided by Airbnb has a search option, but without any prior knowledge of the city and its neighborhoods, narrowing down the options and finding the best deal is a daunting task.

This projects is a Proof Of Concepts (POC) showing how machine learning can be used to give extract insights customers can use to select a neighborhood.

New York as an example¶

With 306 neighborhoods, New York is a perfect example of a city where extra insight on neighborhoods adds value.

Most tourists chose to live in Manhattan, close to Times Square. This is expensive.

If you are wiling to use public transport, other neighborhoods in New York might provide a similar experience for a much lower price.

The result¶

Using a combination of exploratory data analysis and machine learning, three attributes where identified

  • Busy
  • Urban
  • Connected

Each neighborhood in New-York where given a rating from 0 to 10. Combining this with a search API or filter, customers could use the services to find better neighborhoods.

The data¶

The data used is from three sources

  • New York University Spatial database is used to get the name, longitude, latitude and borough for each neighborhood
  • Travel Time is used to get the time it takes from one neighborhood to another using public transport
  • Foursquare provides names, location and venue category for each venue in a neighborhood

Identification of three attributes¶

The quality of the attributes depend on both user understanding and accuracy. If the attributes accurately describes a neighborhood but users don't under stand it or don't find the information relevant when choosing an apartment, it is useless. Likewise, an attribute that is relevant and easy to understand but has not relationship with reality will result in dissatisfied customers.

Attribute 1: Busy¶

The first attribute is the total number of venues in the neighborhood. This variable will be names busy.

It seems like a fair name and it is easy to understand. A transformation might be needed to give values that are easier to communicate.

Attribute 2: Connectivity¶

The first principal component from the travel time weighted venues is named connectivity.

Most of the values from the component are between 0.05 and 0.06. This means a high value for the first component is the same as having many venues reachable with public transport in a short period of time.

Attribute 3: Urban¶

The third attribute is named urban.

The names was chosen based on the positive and negative coefficients in the second principal component from the travel time weighted dataset.

Amongst the venue categories having a coefficient above 0.07 was: Airport Terminal, Baseball Stadium, Beach, Beach Bar, Farm, Field, Golf Course, Outdoors & Recreation. This gives a country feeling

Amongst the venues categories having a coefficient below -0.07 was 12 different types of restaurants. In addition it had venue categories like: Art Museum, Bike Rental, Check Cashing Service, Community Center, Cupcake Shop, Fruit & Vegetable Store, Hostel, Hotel Pool, Indie Theater, Laundromat, Piano Bar, Snack Place, Soup Place. This gives a urban feeling.

Transforming the second principal component by changing the sign gives the third attribute, urban.
